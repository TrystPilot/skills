# Underworld Biographer

A comprehensive skill for crafting rich character biographies, interconnected mythologies, and immersive underworld narratives.

This skill guides you through a structured, three-phase process to develop underworld characters and worlds with historical depth, thematic coherence, and narrative richness.

## The Process

The Underworld Biographer skill follows three integrated phases:

### Phase 1: Character Foundation
Build the fundamental identity and background of your character, establishing who they are before their underworld connection.

### Phase 2: Underworld Integration
Develop the character's relationship with underworld elements, defining their role, motivations, and significance within the larger mythological system.

### Phase 3: Narrative Architecture
Construct the broader mythological system, hierarchies, and narrative framework that contextualizes the character within a complete underworld world.

---

## Phase 1: Character Foundation

### Core Identity Elements

Create a character profile that establishes essential biographical information:

**Name & Titles**
- Primary name and any alternative names or epithets
- Titles earned or inherited
- Significance of their nomenclature

**Origin & Lineage**
- Birth context (mortal, divine, hybrid, created)
- Family connections and lineage
- Formative influences from ancestry
- Any curses, blessings, or inheritances

**Defining Characteristics**
- Physical appearance and distinctive features
- Psychological temperament and personality
- Core values and philosophical positions
- Contradictions or internal conflicts

**Pre-Underworld Life**
- Life circumstances before underworld involvement
- Formative experiences and pivotal moments
- Skills, knowledge, and capabilities developed
- Relationships or allegiances from their former life

### Example Character Foundation

**Character: Malachar the Silent**
- **Name Significance**: "Silent" earned through vow of necessary secrecy
- **Origin**: Born of a forgotten minor nobility, witnessed family's fall to plague
- **Defining Trait**: Possesses perfect recall but chose silence as discipline
- **Pre-Underworld**: Served as archivist and memory-keeper; rejected by court after father's disgrace

---

## Phase 2: Underworld Integration

### Connection & Purpose

Establish how and why the character became part of the underworld:

**The Threshold Moment**
- Specific event or circumstances leading to underworld involvement
- Choice, fate, punishment, or circumstance
- Psychological and emotional impact of the transition
- What was surrendered or gained

**Role Within the Underworld**
- Primary function (guardian, judge, guide, administrator, rebel, etc.)
- Specific responsibilities and sphere of influence
- Relationship to underworld hierarchy and power structure
- Conflicts between personal values and institutional role

**Motivations & Drives**
- Primary motivations for actions within the underworld
- Desires they pursue or struggles against
- Moral ambiguities they navigate
- What they seek to preserve or destroy

**Relationships & Alliances**
- Key relationships with other underworld figures
- Conflicts and rivalries
- Loyalties and obligations
- Leverage points and vulnerabilities through relationships

### Underworld-Specific Attributes

**Powers or Abilities**
- Capabilities unique to underworld existence
- Limitations or costs associated with power
- Evolution of abilities over time
- What they cannot do and why

**Symbolic Significance**
- What they represent within the underworld
- Archetypal role or function
- How others perceive them
- Their personal mythology

## Phase 3: Narrative Architecture

### Mythological Framework

Construct the broader system that contains your character:

**Hierarchical Structure**
- Levels of power and authority within the underworld
- How positions are earned, inherited, or assigned
- Mechanisms of upward or downward mobility
- Exceptions or anomalies to the hierarchy

**Cosmological Rules**
- Fundamental laws governing the underworld
- What is permitted, forbidden, or required
- Consequences of breaking rules
- How these rules shape character motivations

**Historical Narrative**
- Origins of the underworld itself
- Key epochs or transformative events
- Previous conflicts or settlements
- How current characters relate to this history
- What is remembered and what is forgotten

**Thematic Coherence**
- Central theme or question the underworld embodies
- How characters relate to this theme
- Conflicts arising from thematic tensions
- What resolution or transformation is possible

### Interconnected Narrative Web

Map relationships between characters:

**Character Relationships**
- How your character connects to other figures
- Shared history or future entanglements
- Mutual dependencies or conflicts
- Narrative tension points

**Conflict Architecture**
- Primary conflicts your character faces
- External obstacles and internal struggles
- How conflicts interconnect with broader mythology
- Potential resolutions and their costs

**Narrative Trajectories**
- Character arc within the larger story
- Transformation points and implications
- Possible endpoints (redemption, fall, transcendence, stasis)
- How their ending affects others in the mythology

## Working With the Skill

### For Character Development
1. Start with **Phase 1** to establish solid character foundation
2. Move to **Phase 2** to define their underworld role and significance
3. Complete with **Phase 3** to contextualize them within the larger world

### For World-Building
1. Begin with **Phase 3** to establish cosmological rules and hierarchies
2. Use **Phase 2** to populate the world with characters who fulfill essential roles
3. Develop depth in **Phase 1** for your most important characters

### For Narrative Planning
1. Map conflicts in **Phase 3** first
2. Establish character motivations in **Phase 2**
3. Ground decisions in character foundations from **Phase 1**

### Output Formats

Generate content as:
- **Structured documents** (.md files) organizing information hierarchically
- **Character profiles** detailing each phase for reference
- **Mythology primers** explaining the cosmological system
- **Relationship maps** showing interconnections between characters
- **Timeline documents** situating characters within historical narrative

## Key Principles

**Depth Over Novelty**: Prioritize internal coherence and psychological realism over surprising twists. Characters motivated by genuine underworld logic feel more authentic.

**Underworld Logic**: Every rule, role, and relationship should follow from the underworld's own internal logic. Magic or power should have costs and consequences.

**Narrative Function**: Each character should serve a function within the larger narrative. Their presence should matter to the mythological system.

**Ambiguity and Complexity**: Avoid simplistic morality. Underworld characters often occupy ethically complex positions that generate natural narrative tension.

**Interconnection**: The richest underworld narratives emerge when characters are bound together through relationships, shared history, conflicting goals, and mutual dependencies.
